About Me

While an undergraduate at the University of Utah, I was exposed to research in computational electrophysiology and found the science and the involvement in research fascinating. That fascination continued through my graduate studies, during which I studied computer modeling of electrocardiography and defibrillation. I received a PhD in Biomedical Engineering from the University of Utah in 2018. I now work as the technical manager of the Center for Integrative Biomedical Computing (CIBC), part of the Scientific Computing and Imaging (SCI) Institute, managing software and research projects that futher biomedical research with state-of-the art computation tools.
